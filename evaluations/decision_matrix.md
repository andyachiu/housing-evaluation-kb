## Score Rationale

- **Cost:** Current ($4,962), Daly City ($4,715), and San Bruno ($5,115) are cheapest. The Gantry ($7,849) and Mason ($8,565) are most expensive.
- **Hub Proximity:** Distance to Potrero Hill Montessori + 135 Mississippi St. Mason is on Potrero Hill (9). Current/Potrero 1010 are at the base (8). Bernal Heights is close (7). Outer neighborhoods (Portola, Excelsior, Inner Sunset) get 3-4. Peninsula (San Bruno, Daly City) and East Bay (Rockridge) score 1-2.
- **Andy's Commute:** Distance to 121 Spear St. L Seven and 855 Brannan are closest to FiDi. Outer/Suburban comps with good BART access (Excelsior, Daly City, San Bruno, Rockridge) get a 5-6. JC House is worst.
- **Space:** 3BR options score 7-9. Daly City, San Bruno, and JC House lead with garage + yard. All 2BR apartments score 4-5.
- **Condition:** Mason, The Landing, Avalon, and The Gantry score highest. Victorian flats, older SF/Peninsula homes, and JC House score lowest.
- **Neighborhood:** Inner Sunset (Walk 95), Noe Valley (Walk 94), and Rockridge (Walk 88-92) score highest. Suburban options (Daly City, San Bruno) and JC House score lowest.

## Decision

_**Current Apartment (275) and Potrero 1010 (274) lead** on overall value due to low cost and strong hub proximity. **Mason on Mariposa (263)** is the best luxury upgrade but at nearly double cost. **Bernal Heights 3BR Flat (257)** is the top 3BR option with decent hub proximity._

_**Further-Out 3BR Options:** While comps like **Rockridge (220)**, **Daly City (222)**, and **San Bruno (206)** offer great space or savings, they are penalized heavily by poor hub proximity (Kaitlyn's commute and Ethan's school). Without changing schools or accepting a long commute, these are not viable despite their other perks._
